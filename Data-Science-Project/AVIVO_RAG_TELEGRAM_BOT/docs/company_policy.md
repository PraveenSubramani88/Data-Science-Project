# Company Employee Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the rules, responsibilities, and benefits applicable to all employees of the company. The objective is to maintain a professional, productive, and respectful work environment while ensuring compliance with company standards.

## 2. Employment Scope

This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, temporary, contract, and intern employees working for the company. Employees are expected to read, understand, and comply with all provisions outlined in this document.

## 3. Working Hours

The standard working hours are from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with a one-hour lunch break. Employees are expected to be available during business hours and complete assigned tasks within established deadlines.

Any overtime work must be approved by the employee's manager in advance.

## 4. Leave Policy

Employees are entitled to the following leave benefits each calendar year:

* Annual Leave: 25 days
* Sick Leave: 10 days
* Casual Leave: 5 days
* Public Holidays: As announced by the company

Employees must submit leave requests through the company leave management system at least 3 working days before the intended leave date whenever possible.

Sick leave may require medical documentation if the absence exceeds 2 consecutive working days.

Unused annual leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of 5 days into the next calendar year.

## 5. Attendance Policy

Employees must report to work on time and maintain regular attendance.
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week.
Repeated late arrivals, unauthorized absences, or failure to notify supervisors about absences may result in disciplinary action. Employees who are unable to attend work due to emergencies should inform their manager as soon as possible.

## 6. Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to:

* Treat colleagues, customers, and vendors with respect.
* Maintain professional behavior at all times.
* Avoid discrimination, harassment, bullying, or offensive conduct.
* Follow all company policies and procedures.
* Protect the company's reputation and interests.

The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward workplace harassment and discrimination.

## 7. Confidentiality and Data Security

Employees must protect confidential information, including:

* Customer data
* Financial information
* Business plans
* Employee records
* Intellectual property

Confidential information must not be shared with unauthorized individuals inside or outside the company.

Employees must use strong passwords, secure company devices, and report any suspected security incidents immediately.

## 8. Use of Company Assets

Company assets such as laptops, mobile phones, software licenses, internet access, and office equipment are provided for business purposes.

Employees must use company resources responsibly and must not engage in illegal, unethical, or unauthorized activities using company systems.

## 9. Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests.

Any potential conflict of interest, including outside employment, financial investments, or relationships that may influence business decisions, must be disclosed to management.

## 10. Disciplinary Actions

Violations of company policies may result in:

* Verbal warning
* Written warning
* Suspension
* Termination of employment

The severity of disciplinary action will depend on the nature and frequency of the violation.

## 11. Policy Review

The company reserves the right to modify or update this policy at any time. Employees will be notified of significant changes and are responsible for complying with the latest version of the policy.
